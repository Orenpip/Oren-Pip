Cape Cod National

Built a quantitative ML evaluation model to determine which East Coast site should be elevated to National Park status — placing 2nd at UVA's Deloitte First Year Case Competition.

🥈 2nd Place Machine Learning Data Analysis

The Challenge

As part of UVA's Deloitte First Year Case Competition, our team — Jefferson Strategy Group — was asked to evaluate five East Coast candidate sites for elevation to National Park status and deliver a consulting recommendation backed by data. The five candidates were: Cape Cod NS, Delaware Water Gap NRA, Assateague Island NS, Harpers Ferry NHP, and Ocmulgee Mounds NHP.

Our Approach

We built a quantitative evaluation model using LightGBM and XGBoost to score each candidate across four core metrics (visits, spending, jobs, economic output) combined with strategic bonus factors including accessibility, year-round stability, feature diversity, and seasonal balance. All metrics were normalized to 0–100 for objective comparison.

Recommendation

Our analysis recommended Cape Cod National Seashore for elevation to National Park status. It leads all candidates across every economic metric: highest spending per visit ($156/visit), highest output per acre ($17,306), and more jobs supported than every other candidate combined (5,385 jobs).

Annual visitor spending of $598M rivals iconic parks like Glacier and Acadia — and unlike developing sites dependent on projected growth, Cape Cod already operates at full national park scale.

Result

Jefferson Strategy Group placed 2nd out of all competing teams at the Deloitte First Year Case Competition at UVA, February 2026.
