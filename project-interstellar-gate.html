Interstellar Gate

A lunar-orbit space station designed as an exchange hub for the urbanized future of space — enabling steady movement of people, goods, and resources between Earth and deep-space outposts.

Architectural Design 3D Modeling Orbital Mechanics

Overview

Interstellar Gate is a space station in lunar orbit conceived as an interplanetary transit hub — the first purpose-built infrastructure for a future in which space is increasingly urbanized. It enables the steady movement of the people, goods, and resources that far-flung outposts will depend on to function.

Axial Spine

The primary organizing element is a non-rotating axial spine that remains permanently in microgravity. This allows large, non-landing spacecraft to dock, offload cargo, and undergo repair without interacting with any gravity systems. Smaller transfer vehicles interface at intermediate axial levels, enabling staged crew and material movement toward the rotating habitat. By organizing docking axially rather than circumferentially, the station supports simultaneous multi-class traffic.

Habitation Ring & the Dzhanibekov Effect

The segmented rotating ring is designed for long-duration habitation. Within it, four moving masses are integrated to harness the Dzhanibekov Effect. When the masses are equidistant, the ring spins with no tumble — generating artificial gravity through centrifugal force. When moved out of balance, the Effect causes the spine to "flip" axially, repositioning which end of the station faces the Moon on demand, without thrusters.

Decontamination & Access

Access to the habitation ring only occurs through axial modules that progressively increase environmental control. Contamination protocols are enforced by architectural geometry and circulation, not procedure alone.

Launch & Deployment

To accommodate launch vehicle constraints, Interstellar Gate folds into a compact configuration. The central spine and solar panels deploy first. Habitat segments then unfold radially, locking into a continuous ring before spin-up.
