UVA Solar Car Team · Aero-Body Subteam
Solar Car Aerobody

Contributing to the design and fabrication of the solar car's carbon fiber body shell — optimizing external geometry for aerodynamic drag reduction and structural performance under race conditions.

Work involves CFD-informed shape iteration, composite layup planning, and coordination with the chassis and electrical subteams to ensure the body integrates cleanly with all subsystems.

The car competes in multi-day solar racing events, where aerodynamic efficiency directly determines race performance.

UVA Rocketry Team · Air-Brakes Subteam
Rocket Air-Brakes

Designing the air-brake deployment system for UVA's competition rocket in preparation for the International Rocket Engineering Competition (IREC).

Air brakes are drag-inducing fins that deploy during ascent to bleed off velocity and hit a precise target altitude. The system requires real-time actuation based on onboard sensor data, balancing structural loads at high dynamic pressure.

Responsibilities include actuator mechanism design, bracket and deployment geometry, and integration with the flight computer.
