About

I'm a first-year Aerospace Engineering student at the University of Virginia's School of Engineering & Applied Science, pursuing problems that sit at the edge of what's currently possible.

My work spans from NASA drone research to space station architectural design, machine learning competition models, and prosthetic limb development. I'm equally comfortable writing Python, modeling in Fusion 360, and presenting to a boardroom.

I grew up in Philadelphia, competed nationally in science fairs and coding challenges, and am now contributing to UVA's Solar Car Team, Rocketry Team, and CavaLimb prosthetics project simultaneously.

I'm interested in advanced propulsion, structural systems for long-duration spaceflight, and the application of AI to engineering design workflows.
