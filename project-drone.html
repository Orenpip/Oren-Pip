The Build

This drone is entirely custom — the frame was designed from scratch and 3D printed, with the geometry optimized for crash resistance and low weight. The hollow arm lattice reduces mass without sacrificing structural stiffness, and the center plate is designed to cradle the flight stack snugly without additional hardware where possible.

Flight Stack

The electronics are centered around a SpeedyBee F405 V4 flight controller stacked with a 60A 4-in-1 ESC. The all-in-one stack keeps wiring minimal and the center of gravity tight. Four SpeedyBee 1300KV motors spin tri-blade props, producing enough thrust for high-speed freestyle and proximity flying.

The drone is tuned in Betaflight — PID values, filtering, and rates were dialed in through iterative test flights to find the balance between responsiveness and stability.

Design Process

The frame went through multiple iterations. Early versions had arm flex issues under hard maneuvers, which led to a redesign of the arm cross-section and wall thickness. The final design uses a lattice structure along the arms to get stiffness without weight, inspired by aerospace structural optimization methods.

All parts were printed in PLA+ for the prototype phase, with plans to move to PETG for improved impact resistance in future builds.
